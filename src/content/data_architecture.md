Data Architecture Overview

The Samriddhi data architecture is designed around three core principles: data minimization, service ownership, and polyglot persistence. Each application service owns its data and manages its lifecycle independently. Direct database access across services is prohibited; all cross-service interactions occur through secured APIs.

The architecture adopts a polyglot persistence model where data is stored in the technology most appropriate for its access pattern. Transactional and master data are maintained in PostgreSQL, high-volume sensor telemetry is stored in InfluxDB, evidence files are stored in AWS S3, and legally significant incident records are preserved in Hyperledger Fabric.

This approach improves scalability, security, and operational resilience while supporting regulatory requirements and future platform expansion.

Data Storage Strategy

The platform separates operational, analytical, evidence, and blockchain data into dedicated repositories.

PostgreSQL serves as the primary transactional datastore for:

Incident Records
Vendor Profiles
Society Profiles
Compliance Schedules
Evidence Metadata
Insurance Claim Packages

Redis provides low-latency caching for active incidents and frequently accessed operational data.

AWS S3 stores unstructured evidence files including photographs, videos, audio recordings, and generated claim documents. All stored files are encrypted using AES-256 encryption.

InfluxDB manages high-volume IoT telemetry generated by deployed sensors. Its time-series architecture supports efficient ingestion, aggregation, and historical analysis.

Hyperledger Fabric maintains immutable records for high-value incidents requiring legal traceability and insurance verification.

Data Ownership Model

The architecture follows a service-owned data model.

Each service is responsible for:

Creating its own data
Maintaining data quality
Enforcing retention policies
Controlling access permissions

Examples include:

Incident Management Service owns Incident Records.
Vendor Marketplace Service owns Vendor Profiles.
Compliance Tracker Service owns Compliance Schedules.
Insurance Integration Service owns Claim Evidence Packages.

No service is permitted to write directly to another service's database.

Data Lifecycle

The Incident Record acts as the primary business data entity.

The lifecycle begins when a resident submits a complaint through the mobile application. Supporting evidence is stored in AWS S3 and linked to the incident record through metadata.

For incidents classified as high-value, evidence hashes and incident metadata are written to Hyperledger Fabric, creating an immutable legal record.

Throughout the incident lifecycle, status updates, vendor assignments, compliance checks, and resident feedback are recorded until final closure.

Closed incidents are retained for seven years in accordance with legal and operational requirements.

Security and Privacy Controls

The architecture incorporates security controls at multiple layers.

Key controls include:

AES-256 encryption for stored files.
Role-based access control.
NCRID anonymization.
Blockchain hashing for sensitive identity references.
Data residency within India.
Retention-based deletion processes.
Explicit consent management under DPDPA 2023.

All personal data access is governed according to stakeholder roles and business responsibilities.
