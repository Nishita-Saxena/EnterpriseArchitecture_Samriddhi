# Stakeholder Analysis

## 1. Primary Internal Stakeholders

### 1.1 RWA Executives and Office Bearers

**Role:** Society governance and operational decision-making.

**Key Interests**

* Efficient incident resolution
* Transparent financial and operational governance
* Compliance readiness
* Vendor accountability

**Primary Concerns**

* Loss of informal control
* Adoption effort
* Increased visibility into governance practices

**Engagement Approach**

* Position the platform as a governance enablement initiative
* Provide onboarding, training, and dedicated support
* Demonstrate value through audit trails, compliance reporting, and liability protection

---

### 1.2 Residents

**Role:** End users and beneficiaries of community services.

**Key Interests**

* Faster issue resolution
* Transparency in governance
* Privacy protection
* Ease of use

**Primary Concerns**

* Data privacy
* Low adoption motivation
* Digital accessibility challenges

**Engagement Approach**

* Provide multilingual and accessible user experiences
* Deliver visible service improvements early
* Maintain clear privacy and consent practices

---

### 1.3 Property Management Companies (PMCs)

**Role:** Operational management across multiple societies.

**Key Interests**

* Portfolio visibility
* SLA monitoring
* Operational efficiency
* Reduced risk exposure

**Primary Concerns**

* Data ownership
* Integration complexity
* Perceived disintermediation

**Engagement Approach**

* Position Samriddhi as a capability enhancement platform
* Provide portfolio-level dashboards and integration support
* Establish clear data-sharing boundaries

---

## 2. Regulatory and Government Stakeholders

### 2.1 Municipal Corporations

**Role:** Civic administration, compliance oversight, and public service delivery.

**Key Interests**

* Community-level operational visibility
* Improved compliance monitoring
* Better urban planning data
* Smart city alignment

**Primary Concerns**

* Integration complexity
* Data governance
* Resource constraints

**Engagement Approach**

* Begin with low-risk integrations
* Align outcomes with existing digital governance initiatives
* Establish dedicated coordination channels

---

### 2.2 RWA Federations and Apex Bodies

**Role:** Representation and coordination across multiple societies.

**Key Interests**

* Governance standardization
* Collective advocacy
* Operational benchmarking

**Primary Concerns**

* Adoption consistency across member societies

**Engagement Approach**

* Involve federations in governance framework design
* Provide federation-level reporting and analytics
* Leverage federations as rollout partners

---

### 2.3 Insurance Providers

**Role:** Risk assessment and claims processing.

**Key Interests**

* Reliable evidence management
* Faster claims processing
* Improved risk visibility

**Primary Concerns**

* Data quality
* Regulatory compliance
* Legal admissibility of evidence

**Engagement Approach**

* Co-design evidence standards and claims workflows
* Pilot integrations before broader rollout
* Establish governance and compliance controls

---

## 3. Ecosystem and Commercial Stakeholders

### 3.1 Facility Vendors and Service Providers

**Role:** Delivery of maintenance and operational services.

**Key Interests**

* Access to opportunities
* Timely payments
* Transparent performance evaluation

**Primary Concerns**

* Increased accountability
* Competitive pressure
* Digital adoption requirements

**Engagement Approach**

* Provide onboarding support and training
* Introduce transparent performance scorecards
* Implement phased adoption

---

### 3.2 Technology and Infrastructure Partners

**Role:** Platform, cloud, integration, and technology enablement.

**Key Interests**

* Long-term platform growth
* Stable operating environment
* Strategic partnerships

**Primary Concerns**

* Vendor lock-in restrictions
* Architecture governance requirements

**Engagement Approach**

* Define clear SLAs and governance standards
* Maintain open integration principles
* Conduct regular architecture reviews

---

## 4. Community Stakeholders

### 4.1 Senior Residents and Digitally Excluded Users

**Role:** Residents requiring additional accessibility support.

**Key Interests**

* Equal access to community services
* Simple and accessible interactions

**Primary Concerns**

* Digital exclusion
* Usability barriers

**Engagement Approach**

* Provide assisted onboarding
* Support alternative interaction channels
* Incorporate accessibility-focused design principles

---

### 4.2 Media and Public Opinion Groups

**Role:** External influencers of public perception.

**Key Interests**

* Governance transparency
* Community impact outcomes

**Primary Concerns**

* Data privacy incidents
* Negative public perception

**Engagement Approach**

* Maintain proactive communications
* Publish measurable outcomes and success stories
* Establish formal incident communication procedures

---

## 5. Project Governance Stakeholders

### 5.1 Architecture Review Board (ARB)

**Role:** Architecture governance and design assurance.

**Key Interests**

* Architectural consistency
* Standards compliance
* Risk management

**Primary Concerns**

* Technical debt
* Uncontrolled architectural deviation

**Engagement Approach**

* Conduct regular architecture reviews
* Maintain Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)
* Enforce governance and escalation processes

---

### 5.2 Project Sponsor and Funding Body

**Role:** Strategic oversight and programme sponsorship.

**Key Interests**

* Successful programme delivery
* Business value realization
* Scalability and sustainability

**Primary Concerns**

* Delivery risk
* Budget control
* Benefits realization

**Engagement Approach**

* Provide executive reporting and milestone reviews
* Track agreed KPIs and programme outcomes
* Maintain structured governance and change control
