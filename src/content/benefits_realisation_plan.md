# Benefits Realisation Plan

## Purpose

The Benefits Realisation Plan defines the expected business outcomes of the Samriddhi programme, establishes measurable targets, and identifies the organisational and technology changes required to achieve those outcomes. Benefits are monitored throughout the implementation lifecycle and reviewed at each programme phase gate.

---

## Benefit 1: Faster Incident Resolution

### Benefit Statement

Residents receive faster, fully tracked incident resolution through structured workflows, SLA monitoring, and automated notifications.

### Benefit Owner

RWA Committee

### Baseline

Average incident resolution time of 7–14 days with complaint closure rates below 40%.

### Targets

* Month 9: 40% reduction in resolution time and 90% closure rate.
* Month 14: 50% reduction in resolution time and 93% closure rate.
* Month 18: 60% reduction in resolution time and 95% closure rate.

### Measurement

Measured automatically from complaint submission to resident-confirmed closure using platform-generated reports.

### Required Change

Deployment of Incident Management, SLA tracking, vendor assignment, and resident notification capabilities with full platform adoption by residents, committees, and vendors.

---

## Benefit 2: Improved Insurance Claim Acceptance

### Benefit Statement

Insurance claims are supported by verifiable evidence, improving claim acceptance rates and reducing documentation-related rejections.

### Benefit Owner

RWA Committee

### Baseline

Historical claim rejection rates established through pilot-society insurance claim reviews.

### Targets

* Month 14: 80% acceptance rate for blockchain-supported claims.
* Month 18: 90%+ acceptance rate for blockchain-supported claims.

### Measurement

Claim outcomes tracked and compared between blockchain-supported and non-blockchain-supported incidents.

### Required Change

Deployment of blockchain evidence registry and formal recognition of blockchain evidence by participating insurance providers.

---

## Benefit 3: Reduced Legal and Compliance Risk

### Benefit Statement

Comprehensive audit trails and compliance monitoring reduce legal disputes and improve audit readiness.

### Benefit Owner

RWA Committee

### Baseline

62% of societies experience legal disputes annually with limited audit readiness.

### Targets

* Month 14: Legal dispute rate reduced to 40%; 85% audit readiness.
* Month 18: Legal dispute rate below 20%; 95% audit readiness.

### Measurement

Periodic legal dispute surveys and quarterly compliance audit simulations.

### Required Change

Deployment of Compliance Tracker, automated reminders, blockchain evidence management, and complete operational record keeping.

---

## Benefit 4: Operational Cost Reduction

### Benefit Statement

Competitive vendor management and predictive maintenance reduce facility management expenditure.

### Benefit Owner

RWA Treasurer

### Baseline

Annual operating expenditure established during Phase 0 financial assessment.

### Targets

* Month 9: 10% reduction in operating expenditure.
* Month 14: 18% reduction in operating expenditure.
* Month 18: 25% reduction in operating expenditure.

### Measurement

Comparison of pre-implementation and post-implementation operating costs supported by platform transaction and vendor performance data.

### Required Change

Deployment of vendor marketplace capabilities, competitive bidding, performance scorecards, and predictive maintenance services.

---

## Benefit 5: Increased Resident Trust and Engagement

### Benefit Statement

Improved transparency and service responsiveness increase resident participation and community engagement.

### Benefit Owner

RWA Committee

### Baseline

Resident satisfaction of 22%, with baseline adoption, attendance, and fee collection levels established during Phase 0.

### Targets

* Month 9: 55% satisfaction and 60% resident adoption.
* Month 14: 70% satisfaction and 70% resident adoption.
* Month 18: 85% satisfaction and 80% resident adoption.

### Measurement

Resident satisfaction surveys, monthly active user statistics, and fee collection performance.

### Required Change

Deployment of real-time incident visibility, notifications, and resident engagement services.

---

## Benefit 6: Municipal Operational Intelligence

### Benefit Statement

Municipal authorities gain access to real-time operational insights supporting evidence-based governance and resource planning.

### Benefit Owner

Municipal Commissioner’s Office

### Baseline

No structured operational data exchange exists between housing societies and municipal authorities.

### Targets

* Month 14: Municipal dashboard operational for 50 societies.
* Month 18: Municipal dashboard operational for 250 societies with integration to three or more municipal systems.

### Measurement

Dashboard usage metrics, data completeness reporting, integration availability, and stakeholder feedback.

### Required Change

Deployment of municipal integration services and city-level operational analytics.

# Benefits Governance

### Benefits Realisation Manager

Responsible for benefits tracking, reporting, measurement coordination, and escalation of underperforming benefits.

### Benefit Owners

Business stakeholders are accountable for achieving benefits through adoption and operational change. The project team provides enabling technology and support.

### Architecture Review Board (ARB)

Reviews benefits performance at each phase gate and determines readiness for subsequent investment and deployment phases.

---

# Benefits at Risk Management

Where a benefit is projected to miss its phase target, a formal benefits-at-risk assessment shall be submitted to the Architecture Review Board. The assessment will include current performance, root cause analysis, corrective actions, and revised forecasts.

The ARB may:

* Approve corrective actions and retain existing targets.
* Revise targets with documented justification.
* Escalate for additional funding, resources, or scope adjustment.

---

# Measurement Activities

Benefits performance will be monitored through:

* Monthly platform metrics reporting.
* Resident satisfaction surveys.
* Financial performance reviews.
* Legal dispute assessments.
* Compliance audit simulations.
* Insurance claim outcome tracking.
* Architecture Review Board phase-gate reviews.
